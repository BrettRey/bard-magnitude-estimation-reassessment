# Review Board Roster

Reviewer personas for the simulated review board, chosen so each lens lands on a
specific exposed flank of the paper rather than on "acceptability" in general.

## Flank-to-reviewer mapping

| Reviewer (lens) | Stress-tests | Why them |
|---|---|---|
| **Jon Sprouse** | Marginal contribution; is the common-latent-signal null the right model; is a reuse-gated, no-findings paper publishable yet | His data is the empirical spine and he is the acceptability-methodology / statistical-power authority. Also the most likely real referee, so simulating him does double duty. |
| **Antonella Sorace** | Straw-target / fairness: did Bard et al. actually claim ME's *privileged* status, or is the paper deflating a hedged claim | An original Bard co-author. The best check on whether section 2's reconstruction is fair (Rapoport). |
| **Sam Featherston** | Is the ME deflation fair, or does ME retain niche advantages (fine gradience, cross-linguistic scaling) | The strongest ME advocate. Adversarial defense of the method being demoted. |
| **Danielle Navarro / Steven Langsford** | Measurement-model adequacy, reliability overreach, Type S/M usage, forking-path rigor | They ran the reliability/response-style comparison; Bayesian cognitive-modelling rigor. Presses the psychometrics hardest. |
| **Carson Schütze** | Grammaticality vs acceptability level-discipline; whether the construct claims hold | Wrote the line the paper anchors on and the foundational methodology text. Tests the construct spine. |

## Optional adds (section-dependent)

- **Alex Warstadt / Sam Bowman** — if the CoLA/BLiMP afterlife section (section 5) is
  load-bearing, to check that framing and the grammaticality/acceptability
  conflation risk.
- **Ryan Nefdt** — if the projectibility-profile reframing (section 6) is to be
  defended hard; a philosopher of linguistics who will ask whether it does real
  work or is imported HPC scaffolding.

## Recommendation

Run a **core five**: Sprouse, Sorace, Featherston, Navarro, Schütze. Covers
contribution, fairness, adversarial-ME, measurement, and construct, with no two
reviewers redundant. Add Warstadt and/or Nefdt only if section 5 or 6 is where
the most pressure is wanted.

## Open option

Redundant boards: the `/review-board` skill can run parallel Opus and Codex
panels for divergent perspectives, or a single Opus board. Default here is a
single Opus board over the core five.
