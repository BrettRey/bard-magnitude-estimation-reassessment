# Review Board — 2026-07-07

Target: `main.tex` after the approved Sprouse reanalysis and focused
claim-calibration pass.

Board: six independent Codex/GPT reviewers run in parallel: experimental syntax
methodologist, psychometrician/measurement theorist, corpus and benchmark-data
specialist, no-new-data ethics/research-design reviewer, theoretical
syntactician, and projectibility reviewer.

Verdict: unanimous **Revise & Resubmit**. No reviewer recommended rejection.

## Synthesis

### Consensus Strengths

- The paper is fair to Bard et al. Reviewers consistently approved the decision
  to target Bard's scale-resolution claim rather than a straw claim that
  magnitude estimation is a universal gold standard.
- Evidential routing is a major strength. Bard, Sprouse, Langsford, and
  benchmark datasets are kept in different inferential roles rather than pooled
  into a loose literature verdict.
- The response-function null is judged fairer than the earlier common-signal
  framing because bounded scales are allowed to compress extremes before
  magnitude estimation is evaluated.
- The data/permission/AI disclosures are stronger than boilerplate: raw
  participant rows are not redistributed, Sprouse reuse is bounded, and AI-tool
  use is disclosed with author responsibility.
- Section 6 is no longer merely decorative. It separates participant responses,
  expert asterisks, benchmark labels, and the target projectibility profile.

### Consensus Weaknesses

1. **The empirical diagnostics are too compressed.** Several reviewers want item
   counts, transformations, model formulas, uncertainty or sensitivity ranges,
   and a clearer link between each diagnostic and the chartered estimand.
2. **"Practical advantage" needs an operational threshold.** The paper states no
   systematic practical ME advantage, but should say what effect size, contrast
   decision, Type S/M improvement, endpoint discrimination, or noninferiority
   margin would have counted as practically meaningful.
3. **The dimensionality result should stay softer than "gate" language.** The
   implemented analysis is an aggregate PCA/parallel-analysis screen. It cannot
   rule out participant-level method factors, response-style dimensions,
   differential item functioning, or local dependence.
4. **Reproducibility and provenance need more audit detail.** The board wants a
   public reproduction recipe, clearer statement of who approved reuse, and
   softer wording around "prespecified" unless the charter was externally
   locked.
5. **The benchmark afterlife section needs more construction detail.** CoLA,
   BLiMP, and SAD differ in item source, label source, granularity, validation,
   and evaluation target. A compact comparison table would help.
6. **The projectibility payoff should be cashed out earlier and in the
   conclusion.** The paper now has profile and purpose, but should state more
   directly what acceptability judgments, bounded ratings, and magnitude
   estimation license us to project.

### Contradictions and Tensions

- The method reviewers want more operational empirical criteria before the
  conclusion is accepted. The theoretical reviewers want the projectibility
  account to do more explicit inferential work. These are compatible demands:
  operational criteria can be expressed as what each method licenses us to
  project from the observed judgment.
- The no-new-data reviewer wants more auditability, while the permission
  boundary prevents raw-data redistribution. The workable resolution is a public
  reproduction recipe plus aggregate, non-participant-level verification outputs
  if later promoted.

### Self-Check

The board converged very neatly on R&R. Some convergence is expected because the
paper itself advertises its evidential boundaries. Treat the board as a stress
test for likely reviewer reactions, not as a sample of real field consensus.
The strongest signal is cross-role convergence on the same practical fixes:
operational thresholds, reproducibility/provenance detail, and softer
dimensionality language.

## Reviewer Notes

### Experimental Syntax Methodologist

**Summary.** The paper argues that Bard et al.'s durable contribution was making
graded acceptability methodologically serious, but that ME itself does not show
a systematic practical resolution advantage over simpler formal judgment
methods in the available Sprouse data.

**Strengths.** Fair Bard target; disciplined evidential routing; response
functions give ME a fair opportunity to win on Bard's scale-resolution claim.

**Weaknesses.** Diagnostics are too compressed for a methodology venue;
practical advantage lacks an operational threshold; target heterogeneity is not
fully separated from noise, diachronic change, context-sensitive answering,
terminological drift, survey-item mismatch, ambiguity, and confusion.

**Q&A.** What concrete Sprouse-data pattern would have made you conclude that
ME preserved theoretically relevant heterogeneity rather than response-style
variance or item-level mismatch?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.

### Psychometrician / Measurement Theorist

**Summary.** Bard's durable contribution was making graded acceptability
measurable; ME does not show an independent practical resolution advantage over
simpler formal tasks in the available Sprouse comparison data.

**Strengths.** Fair target; strong evidential routing; psychometrically sensible
response-function null.

**Weaknesses.** Aggregate PCA/parallel analysis can show no obvious aggregate
multidimensional divergence, but cannot validate a single latent acceptability
dimension or rule out participant-level method factors, response style, DIF, or
local dependence. The response-function specification needs more detail about
transformations and invariance assumptions. Practical advantage needs a
decision rule or equivalence/noninferiority margin.

**Q&A.** What exact empirical pattern would have counted as a genuine ME
resolution advantage, and can the aggregate dimensionality screen distinguish
absence from aggregation washing out participant-level method structure?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.

### Corpus and Benchmark-Data Specialist

**Summary.** Bard's durable contribution was making graded acceptability
measurable, not establishing ME as practically superior; later benchmarks
inherit only parts of that programme through changed evidential channels.

**Strengths.** CoLA, BLiMP, and SAD are correctly treated as afterlife evidence,
not ME-vs-Likert evidence. Expert labels, participant judgments, benchmark
labels, and the underlying profile are kept separate. SAD is well chosen as a
bridge case.

**Weaknesses.** The benchmark section is too compressed. CoLA, BLiMP, and SAD
should be compared by item source, label source, granularity, validation logic,
and evaluation target. The afterlife claim should emphasize construct change,
not mere extension. The previous "migrated only" wording risked implying direct
historical lineage.

**Q&A.** When CoLA or BLiMP labels an item acceptable/grammatical, what exactly
is the target variable in this framework?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.

### Ethics / No-New-Data Research Design

**Summary.** Bard legitimized graded acceptability as measurable, while ME's
stronger method-specific advantage is not supported by this constrained
secondary reanalysis.

**Strengths.** Clear non-replication boundary; strong evidential-routing table;
better-than-boilerplate data and AI disclosures.

**Weaknesses.** "Author-approved reuse boundary" is too opaque unless the paper
states who approved reuse and what was covered. Reproducibility needs a public
recipe for readers who can obtain the same public files. "Prespecified" needs
provenance and should be softened if the charter was internal rather than
externally locked.

**Q&A.** What can an editor or reviewer verify about reuse permission and the
analysis charter without receiving raw participant rows or private
correspondence?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.

### Theoretical Syntactician

**Summary.** Bard's durable contribution was making graded acceptability
experimentally measurable; ME lacks a demonstrated practical advantage once
response functions, reliability, and evidential channels are kept separate.

**Strengths.** Fair Bard target; valuable grammaticality/acceptability
separation; response-function framing avoids stacking the analysis against
Bard's resolution claim.

**Weaknesses.** "Grammar is a system of projectibility profiles" may overstate
the theoretical burden for a methodology paper and should be framed as an
epistemic account of how grammaticality claims are inferred from evidence. A
second judgment dimension need not reflect grammaticality/register/correction;
it may reflect processing cost, lexical plausibility, scale anchoring, or task
interpretation. Correction behavior is invoked but not operationalized.

**Q&A.** What empirical pattern would support "grammatically licensed but
unacceptable under this task framing" rather than letting one channel decide?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.

### Projectibility Reviewer

**Summary.** Bard's durable contribution was making graded acceptability
measurable, while ME does not earn a practical resolution advantage in the
available secondary evidence.

**Strengths.** The paper separates evidential channels from the target profile.
It gives projectibility inferential content by tying grammaticality claims to
interpretation, production, correction, expert classification, register fit, and
cross-speaker stability. The dimensionality argument is the strongest
projectibility move because the framework predicts where a single acceptability
scalar should fail.

**Weaknesses.** Projectibility arrives structurally late. The conclusion returns
to projectibility profiles but does not fully state what the reassessment now
lets us predict. The Boyd-style slogan has profile and purpose, but not clearly
identified stabilizing mechanisms; if Boyd bears theoretical weight, the source
and stabilizer claim need explicit verification and citation.

**Q&A.** After the Sprouse reanalysis, what is the strongest non-trivial
projection licensed by graded acceptability that would not be licensed by
treating judgments as just another response format?

**Verdict.** Revise & Resubmit.
