# Review Board — 2026-06-23

Target: `main.tex` (full draft, 7 sections). Six independent Opus reviewers, each
saw only the paper. Core five (Sprouse, Sorace, Featherston, Navarro, Schütze)
plus the mandatory Projectibility reviewer. Roster rationale in
`notes/review-board-roster.md`.

**Verdict: unanimous Revise & Resubmit (6/6). No Accept, no Reject.**

## Synthesis

### Consensus strengths

- **Two-claims separation** (acceptability is graded/measurable vs. ME has a
  privileged advantage) is correct, surgical, and fairly executed. All six.
- **Evidential-routing table (§3) with the "claim it cannot support" column** and
  the genre gate: unusually disciplined pre-registration hygiene. Sprouse,
  Sorace, Featherston, Navarro, Schütze.
- **Source-grounding honesty**, including the §2 self-flag that the Bard target
  is not yet page-anchored. Sprouse, Sorace, Navarro, Schütze.
- **§6 projectibility is correctly oriented** (projection-first, explicit
  projection targets, level discipline). Projectibility reviewer; Schütze partly.
- **Schütze's own cited view is represented accurately** (he verified the p. 208
  paraphrase himself: "most or all" preserved, "refuted" not inflated).

### Consensus weaknesses (ranked by how many reviewers flagged, and severity)

1. **The common-latent-signal null begs the question / is rigged against ME.**
   Flagged independently by FIVE of six: Sprouse ("single most important
   conceptual fix"), Sorace ("question-begging"), Featherston ("rigged by
   construction"), Navarro ("pre-supposes the very thing it should test"),
   Projectibility (scalar null contradicts §6's multidimensional profile).
   Modelling ME (unbounded ratio), Likert (bounded ordinal), forced choice
   (binary pairwise), and Thurstonian choice as noisy readings of ONE item-level
   scalar assumes unidimensionality. If bounded scales *censor/compress*
   gradience rather than merely add noise, ME can only ever look like redundant
   noise — the conclusion is baked in. Convergent fix: (a) add method-specific
   *response functions* (compressive/censored link for Likert/FC vs. ratio link
   for ME), not just a noise/bias term; (b) prespecify a *dimensionality test*
   (one-factor vs. correlated-two-factor/bifactor; residual-structure check) that
   can falsify the single-latent assumption, run BEFORE any ME term is
   adjudicated, with the decision rule written into the charter.

2. **The Bard (1996) target is not page-anchored.** Touched by all six; Sorace
   hardest: "you cannot run a fairness argument about a 36-page article you have
   read only in abstract." The §2 TODO admits it. Sorace adds two sub-points: the
   ratio-scale / open-ended-response rationale (the actual methodological core of
   1996) is missing; and the "privileged status" reception is credited to no one
   (cite who held it, or drop the framing).

3. **Marginal contribution / genre.** Sprouse: as written there are no findings;
   pick a lane — Registered Report Stage 1, or a theory/methodology note with §6
   promoted. Name the new estimand the four prior deflations did not deliver;
   "decision stability for theoretically relevant contrasts" is the only genuinely
   underexplored candidate and should be foregrounded, not buried in a five-item
   list.

4. **Type S / Type M are decoration, not method** (Navarro). A *format* does not
   have Type S/M risk; an *estimator of a named contrast at a given N* does.
   Operationalize via design analysis on a specific contrast, or cut.

5. **Projectibility does no inferential work in the ME argument** (Projectibility
   reviewer; Schütze converges). You could delete §6 and §§1–5 would be untouched.
   Boyd's third clause ("projectible *for a purpose*") is missing. The §3 vs. §6
   four-channel inventories are inconsistent; "none is identical to the profile"
   is trivially true. Needs the profile's identity/inference conditions and one
   worked cross-channel example (acceptability projecting, or failing to project,
   to correction/register).

6. **§5 afterlife conflation** (Schütze). "What survived" elides that CoLA labels
   are the *expert/asterisk* channel, not Bard's *naive-participant measurement*
   channel. Split it: portability of contrasts survived into CoLA/BLiMP; graded
   naive-participant measurement migrated only into crowdsourced SAD.

7. **Target heterogeneity** (Navarro). The single-latent model cannot distinguish
   *stable* disagreement (dialect, register, diachronic change across the
   1996/2013/2017 waves, ME modulus/anchoring effects) from noise. Say what
   heterogeneity it preserves vs. smooths; add an item-by-population variance
   component.

8. **B/G/M exclusion unverified** (Sprouse). The practice-item characterization is
   asserted, not confirmable from the manuscript. Resolve in the same permission
   exchange.

### The key convergence (most important insight)

The top **methodological** objection (#1, rigged null), the top **theoretical**
objection (#5, projectibility decorative), and the **contribution** objection (#3)
have the SAME fix. Projectibility's multidimensional profile *predicts where the
single-scalar latent model should break* — items where acceptability diverges from
correction, register fit, or cross-speaker stability. Building that into a
prespecified dimensionality/response-function test would simultaneously: (a)
de-rig the null, (b) make §6 load-bearing on the empirical core, (c) supply
Boyd's missing "for a purpose," and (d) give the reanalysis a genuinely new
estimand (method-construct divergence) the four prior papers did not address.
Five reviewers from divergent priors — an ME defender, a measurement
statistician, the data owner, an original author — converged on the null problem;
that cross-prior agreement is the strongest signal in the board.

### Contradictions / tensions

- **Sprouse** says lean toward the theory note (the gated reanalysis may be
  "decorative" if no result could flip the verdict). **Projectibility + Schütze**
  say the theory half (§6) is itself currently decorative. Net: *neither half is
  yet load-bearing alone* — the empirical half is gated/possibly-redundant, the
  theory half generates no prediction. The convergent fix above is what resolves
  the bind.
- **Featherston** wants the null re-specified so ME *can* win (advocacy);
  **Navarro/Sprouse** want it re-specified so the inference is *valid* (neutral).
  Same fix, different motive — a convergence, not a clash.

### Self-check (do not over-read the unanimity)

Some convergence is inflated by the paper advertising its own gaps: the §2 TODO
told every reviewer the Bard target was unanchored, so that is one disclosed
weakness picked up six times, not six independent discoveries. The
ratio-vs-ordinal null critique is also somewhat "in the water" for anyone who
thinks about scale types. Treat the board as a stress test for likely real-referee
reactions, not as a silicon sample of field consensus. The genuinely load-bearing
signal is the *cross-prior* agreement on the null (#1) and the *independent*
agreement of the construct reviewers that §6 does not yet generate a prediction.

### Prioritized revision list (by impact)

1. Re-specify the null (method-specific response functions) and prespecify a
   dimensionality test that can falsify unidimensionality, with the decision rule
   in the charter. [#1]
2. Make §6 projectibility generate that dimensionality prediction; add Boyd's "for
   a purpose"; unify the theory and empirical halves. [#5 + the key convergence]
3. Page-anchor the Bard target from the primary article; engage the ratio-scale
   rationale; name or drop the "privileged status" reception. [#2] (Needs the 1996
   PDF in `literature/`.)
4. Pick a genre lane and name the new estimand (foreground decision stability for
   theoretically relevant contrasts). [#3]
5. Operationalize or cut Type S/M; tighten §5 afterlife channel split; reconcile
   the §3/§6 channel inventory and give the profile identity conditions + one
   worked example; address target heterogeneity with an item-by-population term.
   [#4, #6, #5, #7]
6. Resolve the B/G/M exclusion with Sprouse in the permission exchange. [#8]

## Full reviews

### 1. Jon Sprouse (acceptability methodology / statistical power)

**Summary:** Pry apart the durable claim (acceptability graded/measurable) from
the separable, unearned one (ME privileged); lays out a prespecified,
permission-gated reanalysis against a common-latent-signal null.

**Strengths:** construct disentangling is right and not a strawman; §6 level
discipline is the key conceptual contribution; the §3 routing table with explicit
"cannot support" column is exemplary pre-registration hygiene; the data discipline
around his files is honest and accurate.

**Weaknesses:** (a) marginal contribution beyond the construct reframing is thin
and the paper half-admits it — needs one paragraph stating what the reanalysis
shows that Sprouse 2013/2017, W&F 2011, Langsford 2018 did not; "decision
stability for theoretically relevant contrasts" is the only underexplored
estimand and should be foregrounded. (b) The common-latent-signal null may beg the
question — it assumes one latent signal; needs a falsifier for the single-latent
assumption (dimensionality check, method-factor loadings, residual structure), not
only a rule for when a method term is "interesting." (c) §3 representation choices
mostly defensible; pair-level vs logistic are not independent evidence and
shouldn't be reported as convergent corroboration; the B/G/M exclusion is a
plausible but *unverified* claim about his data — flag it, resolve it in the
permission exchange.

**Q&A:** If you run the analysis and ME is just a noisier reading of the same
signal, what do you claim that the four prior papers did not establish? What
single result would make you say ME *was* necessary? If none, the reanalysis is
decorative.

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit (lean weak). Genre crux: today it is a hybrid of a
Registered Report Stage 1 and a theory/synthesis note — pick a lane. Nothing in
the manuscript misrepresents his data or the contact caveat.

### 2. Antonella Sorace (original Bard co-author)

**Summary:** Bard et al. bundled a durable claim and a weaker separable one; the
paper sets up a permission-gated reanalysis to test whether ME's special status
survives.

**Strengths:** level discipline is genuinely good and the real contribution;
pre-registration ethic serious and honestly applied; the reassessment is not a
hatchet job and credits the 1996 programme.

**Weaknesses:** (a) the target is unanchored and the paper knows it — you cannot
run a fairness argument about a 36-page article read only in abstract; read it and
page-anchor every attributed claim. (b) The "privileged status" claim is a
reception artifact credited to no one — name who held it or drop the framing,
which cuts against the paper's own fairness thesis. (c) The reassessment
underweights what was load-bearing in 1996 because it has only read the abstract —
the ratio-scale / open-ended-response rationale (why ME was imported from
psychophysics: bounded Likert imposes a ceiling and an interval assumption ME's
unbounded ratio scale escapes) appears nowhere, nor the L2/cross-group gradience
motivation.

**Q&A:** In 1996 "graded & measurable" and "ME specifically" were entangled for a
principled reason — the open-ended ratio scale was the instrument by which we
argued gradience is real signal, not an artifact of a coarse format. Is your
common-latent null question-begging when ME and Likert disagree because they
instantiate different measurement models, not as noise around a shared latent?

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit.

### 3. Sam Featherston (ME advocate, adversarial defense)

**Summary:** Separates Bard's durable contribution from the weaker ME-advantage
claim; proposes a gated reanalysis against a common-latent null; routes benchmarks
to a separate afterlife channel.

**Strengths:** two-claims split is useful and fair (concedes he has himself let
the gradience and format points ride together); evidential routing is honest;
structural inclusion rules are appropriately pre-committed.

**Weaknesses:** (a) the paper conflates reliability with resolution/validity and
assumes the trade-off away — "less reliable" (Langsford) is not "no advantage"; ME
may trade reliability for sensitivity to gradience Likert compresses
(ceiling/floor censoring is not a noise problem). Add a subsection distinguishing
reliability / validity / resolution / freedom from scale-boundary censoring. (b)
The common-latent null is structurally pre-committed against the pro-ME claim —
needs a method-specific *response function* (censored/compressive link for bounded
scales vs ratio link for ME); otherwise the no-method-advantage negative control
tests a strawman. (c) No defensible ME niche acknowledged — end-of-scale
compression, ratio comparisons, cross-item/cross-language scaling. Add a "where ME
still earns its keep" passage.

**Q&A:** If the genuine pro-ME claim is that bounded scales censor/compress
gradience rather than merely add noise, your null has no parameter that could
register that advantage — how is it not rigged? Will you add a method-specific
response function and report *that* as the real test?

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit.

### 4. Danielle Navarro (Bayesian measurement / reliability)

**Summary:** Bard's durable contribution was making graded acceptability
measurable, not ME specifically; gated reanalysis models four methods as noisy
readings of one item-level signal with any ME term forced to earn its place.

**Strengths:** two-claim separation useful and fairly executed (honest §2 TODO);
the routing table and genre gate are the best part — sources declare what they
*cannot* support; prespecification has real teeth (primary vs sensitivity by unit
of analysis; structural B/G/M exclusion).

**Weaknesses:** (a) the single-latent null pre-supposes unidimensionality and
never specifies a check that could falsify it — needs a prespecified one-factor vs
correlated-two-factor/bifactor comparison and residual-correlation examination
*before* any ME term is adjudicated, or "ME must earn its place" is rigged. (b)
Type S/M are decoration — a format does not have Type S/M risk; an estimator of a
named contrast at a given N does; operationalize via design analysis or cut. (c)
**Target-heterogeneity unaddressed** — a single-latent + method-noise model cannot
distinguish stable between-speaker/diachronic/anchoring variation from error; say
what heterogeneity the method preserves vs suppresses; carry item-by-population
variance / item-by-method interaction. Connects to §6's own warning against
channel-collapse.

**Q&A:** What single prespecified diagnostic, run before looking at any ME term,
would tell you the common-latent null is the wrong measurement model rather than
ME the wrong winner — and where in the charter is that decision rule written?

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit. Langsford limitation handled honestly (no
load-bearing number imported); keep it that way.

### 5. Carson Schütze (grammaticality vs acceptability)

**Summary:** Bard's durable contribution was making acceptability measurable; ME's
method-specific advantage is separable and largely unearned; acceptability is one
evidential route into grammar as a system of projectibility profiles.

**Strengths:** two-claims split correct and well-drawn; **his own position is
represented accurately** — verified the p. 208 paraphrase ("most or all"
preserved, not inflated; the Bard characterization honestly flagged as not yet
primary-anchored); routing table and genre gate methodologically serious.

**Weaknesses:** (a) the channel inventory is inconsistent between §3 ("four
evidential routes") and §6 (participant responses / expert asterisks / benchmark
labels / theoretical grammaticality) — three are observation types, the fourth is
the *target*; "none is identical to the profile" is then trivially true. Give the
profile identity conditions independent of the evidence, or drop the
co-ordinate-routes claim. (b) "Acceptability is one evidential route into a
projectibility profile" needs a test or it is a relabeling of competence/
performance — Goodman is cited but the paper never says what observing
acceptability licenses you to predict about the other channels; give one worked
example. (c) §5's "what survived" elides that CoLA labels are the expert/asterisk
channel, not naive-participant measurement — split portability-of-contrasts from
graded measurement (the latter migrated only into crowdsourced SAD).

**Q&A:** When two channels disagree (low naive acceptability, unstarred in the
literature, attested in a register corpus), what fixes the verdict about the
profile? If nothing, how is "the profile" more than the unweighted set of
channels?

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit. Schütze 2016 anchor verified accurate.

### 6. Projectibility reviewer (HPC countermeasure)

**Summary:** Bard's durable contribution was measurable graded acceptability; ME's
advantage must be earned against a common-latent null; acceptability is one route
into a projectibility profile.

**Strengths:** §6 is correctly oriented — projection-first, explicit projection
targets, not name-dropped; level discipline is in service of projection and does
real demarcation; the acceptability/ME relocation is conceptually load-bearing for
the central separation (deflating ME does not deflate the construct).

**Weaknesses:** (a) Boyd's third clause — "projectible *for a purpose*" — is
essentially absent; state the purpose (theoretical syntax? TESL?). (b)
Projectibility does NO inferential work in the ME argument — delete §6 and §§1–5
are untouched; it licenses no prediction about ME or the datasets the
common-latent null doesn't already deliver. (c) The common-latent scalar null and
the multidimensional profile are unreconciled — if the profile is genuinely
multidimensional, projectibility should predict *where the single-scalar model
breaks down* (items where acceptability and correction/register diverge); that is
a real generable prediction left on the table.

**Q&A:** Name one prediction the projectibility framing licenses about the
Sprouse/Langsford data — about ME, or where the single-scalar model should fail —
that you could not state from the common-latent null alone. If you can't, what is
§6 doing besides relabeling?

**Verdict:** Revise & Resubmit — projectibility is load-bearing only for the
conceptual split and decorative for the empirical contribution; supply "for a
purpose" and make it generate one concrete prediction, or §6 reads as imported
scaffolding.
