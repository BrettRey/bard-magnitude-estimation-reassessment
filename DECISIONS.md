# Decisions

## 2026-06-23

- Use a lower-case folder name: `bard-magnitude-estimation-reassessment`.
- Treat the project as a secondary-data reassessment, not a direct replication, unless the original Bard et al. participant-level data is found.
- Use Bard, Robertson, and Sorace (1996) as the conceptual target.
- Use later method-comparison work as the empirical base after verification.
- Use contemporary acceptability datasets as the construct's afterlife, not as direct replacements for magnitude-estimation data.
- Use afterlife sources rhetorically for the survival and transformation of formal acceptability measurement, not evidentially for ME-vs-Likert.
- Keep house style strictly central by symlink. Do not copy local house-style files.
- Keep candidate sources in `notes/source-verification.md` until verified.
- Cite Bard, Robertson, and Sorace (1996) with DOI `10.2307/416793`, while noting that the Edinburgh record still reports no DOI.
- Treat CoLA labels as expert/literature acceptability-grammaticality labels, not as naive-speaker acceptability judgments.
- Treat the Syntactic Acceptability Dataset as a bridge case because it explicitly separates literature-extracted grammaticality from crowdsourced acceptability.
- Treat the source-grounded Bard target as the claim that ME solves scale problems in conventional methods and yields robust fine-grained acceptability data. Do not attribute a broader superiority claim to Bard unless the full article or a reception source is cited for that stronger reading.
- Treat Sprouse et al. (2013) and Sprouse and Almeida (2017) as sources of public row-level method-comparison data from Jon Sprouse's current author site, with the stated author-contact caveat for novel research reuse.
- Do not treat Bard or Langsford as sources of public participant-level data unless an author-controlled or publisher-linked repository is found.
- Use a Gelmanian measurement null as the empirical comparison where the available data can support it: magnitude estimation has no practical measurement advantage once item acceptability, task noise, scoring choices, and any estimable participant or item-by-method terms are accounted for.
- Treat scrambled labels, method-label permutations, and no-method-advantage simulations as negative controls for the pipeline, not as the substantive null.
- Do not model participant response style, test-retest reliability, or method-by-item instability from published rates alone. Those estimands require raw participant-level rows.
- Use `notes/analysis-charter.md` as the pre-analysis commitment device. Primary datasets, comparisons, scoring rules, practical-advantage criteria, negative controls, and forbidden claims should not change after inspecting new row-level results unless the change is labelled exploratory or a revision trigger occurs.
- Proceed with Sprouse structural data inventory while reuse is pending, but avoid method rankings, convergence estimates, model fits, or publication-ready novel results until the reuse question is resolved or the analysis is reframed as verification/reproduction.
- Preserve original Sprouse identifiers in scripts. Spacing-normalized keys may be used only as explicit candidate crosswalk fields, not as silent recoding.
- Use `FC.signtest.csv` as the primary 2013 forced-choice representation because it is already pair-level and aligns with the pair-level ME/LS crosswalk. Use `FC.logistic.csv` only as a named sensitivity representation.
- Exclude 2017 yes/no item IDs `B`, `G`, and `M` from item-level comparisons because they are absent from the materials workbook, appear at repeated early order positions, and do not align with the ME/LS/FC item universe.
- Require the Sprouse analysis gate to pass before any substantive outcome script is treated as primary. While reuse is pending, the gate may verify structure but should keep method rankings, convergence estimates, means, and model fits closed.
- Position the paper against the existing ME-critique literature in section 1, so the marginal contribution is the construct-level reframing and the prespecified estimand routing, not the bare verdict that ME has no special advantage. Reason: Schütze (2016: 208) already records that ME's advantages over Likert have "most or all" been refuted; without this positioning a reviewer would call the empirical conclusion redundant.
- Add Weskott and Fanselow (2011) as `weskott2011` in `references-local.bib`, with metadata copied from Schütze (2016) and a comment flagging that issue number and DOI still need primary verification before `/push-bib`. Reason: it is the canonical anti-ME result and the paper must cite it directly, but its primary page has not yet been verified.
- Reframe the abstract so the Sprouse secondary analysis reads as prespecified and gated on reuse permission, not as already performed. Reason: TESL scope dropped and Sprouse reuse permission is still pending, so the paper is currently a design and synthesis piece.
- Drop the TESL-facing framing from the manuscript by decision (Brett, 2026-06-23). The paper is a pure experimental-syntax measurement reassessment.
- Verify Weskott and Fanselow (2011) against Crossref: *Language* 87(2), 249--273, DOI `10.1353/lan.2011.0041`. `weskott2011` updated and now ready for `/push-bib`.
- Establish a simulated review board with a core five (Sprouse, Sorace, Featherston, Navarro, Schütze), each lens mapped to a specific exposed flank. Roster saved in `notes/review-board-roster.md`. Reason: covers contribution, fairness, adversarial-ME defense, measurement rigor, and construct discipline without redundancy.
- Obtained and read the full Bard, Robertson & Sorace (1996) article (now in `literature/`); page-anchored §2 from the primary text and resolved the §2 TODO. Reason: the review board (Sorace especially) flagged that a fairness argument cannot rest on an abstract-only reconstruction.
- Correct the §2 framing of Bard's claim: Bard et al. do make a method-superiority claim, but a specific one, namely that an open ratio-yielding scale recovers gradient distinctions that fixed-point scales censor by construction (a resolution / scale-boundary argument, pp. 32, 35, 41). The broader "ME is the privileged formal method in general" is a reception extrapolation, not Bard's claim; their comparison class is informal annotation and fixed-point scales, not the later Likert/forced-choice/yes-no/Thurstonian inventory. Reason: the primary text supports a sharper and more defensible target than "privileged status is a pure reception artifact."
- Note the live seam this opens: Bard's actual claim is about RESOLUTION (fixed scales censor distinctions), which the current common-latent-signal null (framed around convergence/reliability) cannot test. This is the same gap five reviewers flagged; resolving it is the pending null-respecification task.
- Re-specify the empirical null from "common signal plus method noise" to a RESPONSE-FUNCTION measurement model: methods read a common item-level signal through method-specific links (unbounded ratio for ME; ordered-threshold/Thurstonian/binary for the bounded tasks), so scale-boundary censoring is represented in the model rather than absorbed as ME noise. Reason: five of six reviewers (from opposite priors) judged the plain common-signal null rigged against ME, and Bard's actual claim is a resolution claim the plain null cannot register.
- Add a dimensionality gate run BEFORE any ME-specific term: compare one-factor vs correlated-two-factor and bifactor models and check method-residual structure; if methods load on distinct dimensions, the single-signal comparison is the wrong question. This is the prespecified falsifier for unidimensionality. Decision rule written into `notes/analysis-charter.md`.
- Make resolution a first-class estimand and practical-advantage criterion, distinct from reliability (Langsford's "less reliable" does not settle resolution). Fix the Type S/M framing in §4: Type S/M are properties of estimating a named contrast at a given N, not of a format.
- Make §6 projectibility load-bearing: the multidimensional profile predicts WHERE the single-scalar model should break (items where acceptability diverges from correction/register/cross-speaker stability), which is the §3 dimensionality gate. Added Boyd's "projectible for a purpose" clause. Reason: the board judged §6 decorative ("delete it and the empirical argument is untouched"); this ties it to the method comparison and unifies the theory and empirical halves.
- Propagate the re-specified null to §3, §4, §7, `notes/project-brief.md`, and `notes/assumptions-and-pressure-test.md`. The analysis stays gated on Sprouse permission; this is design/charter prose only, no results.
- Split the §5 afterlife claim by channel: what survived into CoLA/BLiMP is the portability of contrasts via the expert-asterisk channel, NOT Bard's naive-participant graded measurement, which migrated only into crowdsourced resources like SAD. Reason: Schütze flagged that §5's undifferentiated "what survived" credited the acceptability-measurement programme with an afterlife that is partly the expert-judgment programme's.
- Give the projectibility profile explicit identity conditions in §6: the profile is the structure of inferences a form licenses (the fourth thing, distinct from the three observation channels), it is not the average of channels, and channel disagreement is adjudicated by which inferences actually hold, not by a vote. Reason: Schütze's "none is identical to the profile" was trivially true without identity conditions; this answers "how is the profile more than the unweighted set of channels."
- Disambiguate the "evidential routes" collision: §3 now says "four kinds of source" (routed to estimands) and cross-references §6's evidential channels (into a profile), so the two senses no longer clash.
- House-style fix: "data" is singular (main.tex and §3 had "data are"); now "data is".
